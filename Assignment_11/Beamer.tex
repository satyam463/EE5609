\section{Problem Statement}
 Verify that the set of complex numbers numbers described in the form of $x + y\sqrt{2}$ where x and y are rational is a sub-field of C.
 \section{Solution}
 Lets consider the subfield S = $x + y\sqrt{2}$  
 We must verify that S meets the following two conditions:
 \begin{align}
     0 , 1 \in S\label{cond1} \\
     If a,b \in S , a+b,-a,ab,a^{-1} \in S\label{cond2}
 \end{align}
 Throughout let 
 \begin{align}
 a=x+y\sqrt{2}, b=w+z\sqrt{2}
 \end{align}
 \begin{align}
 if x=0 = y=0 \in Q , a=0+\sqrt{2}.0 = 0 , 0\in S\\ 
 if x=1 , y=0 , a=1+\sqrt{2}.0 =1 , 1 \in S
 \end{align}
 Hence \refeq{cond1} is verfied .
 \begin{align}
 a+b = x+y\sqrt{2}+w+z\sqrt{2} = b+a\\
 -a = -x-y\sqrt{2} , x,y\in Q so -x , -y \in Q , a \in S\\
 ab=(x+y\sqrt{2})(w+z\sqrt{2})=ba , ab \in S\\
 a^{-1} a = (x+y\sqrt{2})^{-1}(x+y\sqrt{2})=1, a^{-1} \in S
\end{align}
Hence \refeq{cond2} is verfied .
